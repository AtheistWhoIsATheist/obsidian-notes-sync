## CYCLE 1 — TERMINOLOGY CONSOLIDATION & ZERO-AMBIGUITY GLOSSARY

Operational definitions (succinct, discriminative, first-use ready):

- Nihilism: Global or domain-specific denial of inherent meaning, value, or teleology (ontological, axiological, epistemic variants).
- Nihiltheism: Working hypothesis that radical Nothingness can function as an aperture for Transcendent Resonance (TR)—a non-appropriable disclosure that reorients lived existence without committing to positive theism.
- Transcendent Resonance (TR): Experiential disclosure marked by affective intensity (numinous awe/dread), cognitive decentering (ego dissolution), and ethical reorientation; ontologically non-coercive and interpretively underdetermined.
- UNE (Universal Nihilistic Event): Cross-cultural, repeatable experiential pattern: confrontation with groundlessness → interpretive bifurcations (secular, mystical, aesthetic, pathological).
- Apophatic Discipline (AD): Methodological constraint that forbids positive ontological predication when warrant is lacking; privileges negative, structural, or topological descriptions.
- Phenomenal Sufficiency: Begin from what is experientially given; theory earns entry post-hoc.
- Zero-Predicate Constraint (ZPC): Formal rule: No positive divine predicate appears unflagged; any metaphor is labeled non-assertoric.
- Non-Evasion Norm: Disallow consolatory inferences that exceed evidence; spotlight ambiguity rather than mask it.
- Meontology: Investigation of non-being as structurally/causally potent within accounts of becoming or disclosure.

- **Apophasis**: Saying-by-unsaying; disciplined negation that avoids reifying “Nothingness” as a thing.

- **Kenosis**: Self-emptying; ego/meaning-structures relinquished to expose the field of givenness.

- **Absurd Perspective**: The clash between the will-to-meaning and an indifferent or silent cosmos.
