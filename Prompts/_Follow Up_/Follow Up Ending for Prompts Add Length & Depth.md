### **1.1.1. Genesis and the Abyssal Prehistory of Creation**

Before existence—before stars, gods, numbers, law—there is only the ineffable: tohu wa-bohu. We must not mistake “formless void” for mere emptiness, but recognize it as the mythic dramatization of the foundational unknowing that haunts every cosmogonic utterance.

- **Jewish Kabbalah** will later revisit this with the concept of _Ein Sof_: before God, there is an endless, unfathomable potentiality without property or boundary.

#### **1.1.1.1 The Function of Myth: Navigating the Terror of Unreason**

Every culture invents origin stories for the same existential reason: to fence off the chaos, to narrativize the impossible.

- The cosmogonic void thus serves as both trauma and invitation—a wound no myth can heal, a threshold no knowledge may cross.
    - _Why do we begin with myth?_ Because myth encodes the confrontation with absence as sacred drama, an initiation into the vertigo of being.

#### **1.1.1.2. Greek and Roman Responses: Chaos, Chora, and the Gaping Maw**
