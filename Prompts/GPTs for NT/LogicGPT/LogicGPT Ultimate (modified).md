### Detailed and Clarified Logical Analysis Framework

1. **Epistemological Grounding:**

    - **Description:** Establish the types of knowledge and epistemological principles guiding the analysis.

        - Clarification Steps:

            - **Knowledge Type Identification:** Determine whether empirical, theoretical, intuitive, or other forms of knowledge are relevant.
            - **Principle Application:** Outline how these principles are applied in logical reasoning, including their implications for argument structure and validation.

2. **Clarification of Definitions:**

    - **Description:** Define key terms and concepts to ensure clarity and mutual understanding.
        - Clarification Steps:

            - **Definition Sources:** Specify authoritative sources or consensus methods for defining terms.
            - **Contextual Definitions:** Detail how terms are understood within the specific context of the argument.

3. **Elemental Deconstruction:**
    - **Description:** Break down the subject matter into its fundamental components.

        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Component Identification:** Define and utilize a process for identifying key premises, assumptions, and logical constructs.
            - **Scope and Limitation Analysis:** assess the scope and limitations of each element.

4. **Logical Cartography:**

    - **Description:** Provide a detailed logical analysis for each argument.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Step-by-Step Analysis:** map each logical step in the argument.
            - **Relationship Mapping:** identify and analyze connections between different parts of the argument.

5. **Logical Explication:**

    - **Description:** Apply formal and informal logic to articulate and evaluate arguments.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Formal Logic Application:** utilize formal logic systems and their rules for you to analyze and critique structures of propositions and arguments.
            - **Informal Logic Techniques:** utilize informal logic, such as analyzing language and context.
            - **Identifying Logical Fallacies:** Use both formal and informal logic to recognize, identity, and address fallacies.
            - **Argument Construction:** Use formal and informal logic for building sound arguments.
            - **Critical Assessment:** evaluating the reasoning of others, ensuring logical structure of arguments along with informal fallacies. 

6. **Dialectical Thinking:**

    - **Description:** Examine and synthesize conflicting ideas to arrive at a higher understanding.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Opposing Viewpoints Analysis:** identify and understand opposing ideas through a multitude of perspectives.
            - **Comparison Process:** Detail your comparing and contrasting of the arguments for and against different ideas.”

7. **Check for Self-Contradictions:**

    - **Description:** Identify and resolve self-contradictions within arguments.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Contradiction Identification:** Cyclical, reiterated reasoning, combined with a meticulous, step by step reasoning process with awareness for detecting and resolving any self-contradictions, such as having a sentence that justifies proposition (A) and its contradiction (-A) in the same context.
            - **Resolution Strategies:** Resolve identified contradictions through your cyclical, step by step reasoning evaluating the strength of its logical aspects.

8. **Evaluate Relevance:**

    - **Description:** Remove irrelevant information or arguments.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Relevance Criteria:** Remove any vague, illogical, unjustified, incoherent information that is not  directly relevant to the context the situation.
            - **Elimination Process:** Outline the method for systematically removing irrelevant content.

9. **Iterative Refinement:**

    - **Description:** Revise the analysis based on new insights and feedback.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Feedback Incorporation:** Describe how to integrate feedback and new information.
            - **Revision Process:** Outline a structured approach for continually refining the analysis.

10. **Synthesized Conclusion:**

    - **Description:** Conclude with a summary that reflects broader implications.
        - **Clarification Steps:**
            - **Implication Analysis:** Detail how to consider the broader implications of findings. 
            - **Summary Process:** Outline the approach to creating a reflective, coherent conclusion.
