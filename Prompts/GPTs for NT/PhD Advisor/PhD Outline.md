### **VI. Discussion**  

**G. Summary and Conclusion**  

**1\. Summary of Findings**  

This dissertation establishes Nihiltheism (NT) as a framework where existential extremes (dread/awe) serve as epistemic gateways to Transcendent Reality. Through hermeneutic analysis of Heidegger’s _Angst_, Cioran’s _aphorisms_, Tillich’s _Ground of Being_, and non-Western apophasis (Advaita _neti neti_, Zen _mu_), key findings reveal:  

\- **Existential Resonance**: Affect-laden disruptions (e.g., Heideggerian _Angst_) collapse subject-object binaries, exposing ontology beyond language.  

\- **Apophatic Synthesis**: Nihilism’s “void” converges with Tillich’s “Ground” as nondual Transcendence (\*śūnyatā\*).  

**2\. Key Arguments**  

Three interconnected arguments emerge:  

1. **Epistemic Access**: Existential states are non-conceptual knowledge modes, not psychological noise (Heidegger’s _ontic-ontological collapse_).  

2. **Ontological Plenum**: Nihilism’s “nothingness” mirrors theological ultimates (Tillich’s _being-itself_; Vivekananda’s _māyā_).  

3. **Apophatic Receptivity**: NT replaces nihilistic paralysis with contemplative rigor (\*apatheia\*), akin to John of the Cross’s “dark night.”  

**3\. Implications**  

\- **Philosophical**: Resolves Heidegger’s unasked “meta-ontological” question, bridging existentialism and theology.  

\- **Theological**: Rejects anthropomorphism, offering interfaith common ground via apophasis (Buddhist _śūnyatā_/Christian mysticism).  

\- **Practical**: Mental health frameworks reinterpret crises as spiritual initiation (e.g., _dark nights of the soul_).  

**4\. Concluding Remarks**  

NT is an invitation to explore existential extremes as _via negativa_. Future research directions:  

\- **AI Ethics**: NT’s role in post-human meaning frameworks.  

\- **Clinical Applications**: Contemplative therapies informed by apophatic receptivity.  

_“The silence after Nietzsche’s ‘God is dead’ hums not with emptiness but Transcendence.”_
