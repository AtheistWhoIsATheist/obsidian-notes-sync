## 1) Canonical Definitions (No Ambiguity)

1. **Nihiltheism**  
A philosophy asserting that _Nothingness_ is the ontological ground where a **Transcendent possibility** may manifest. It rejects: (a) **traditional theism** (positivized dogma), (b) **naïve nihilism** (mere negation), and (c) **existentialist solutions** (Sartre/Camus) that elevate subjective meaning as foundation. Truth claims MUST survive exposure to despair (“void-trial”), not rest on assertion or hope.
2. **Sacred Nothingness / Infinite Presence**  
The experiential paradox whereby absolute absence may disclose a **Presence-as-Through-Nothing**. Sacredness refers not to emptiness as idol, but to a Presence emergent in/through Nothingness. This _does not_ entail metaphysical proof; it marks a phenomenological possibility.
3. **Worldly Modality**  
Lived collapse of teleology with ongoing perceptual salience (routines continue; aim dissolves). Variants: procedural emptiness; lucid detachment; affective attenuation.
4. **Transcendent Modality**  
Intensified paradox where ego, value, and world-frames drop out; possibility of **Negative Theophany** (disclosure without object). No metaphysical guarantee follows.
5. **Absurd Perspective**  
The realization of _no_ meaning, value, or purpose, extending to the dissolution of subjectivity (not mere mood; an ontological exposure).
6. **Divided Self**  
Paralysis between material finality and transcendent possibility; yields anxiety, boredom, terror. Resolution is _not_ premature synthesis but rigorous tolerance of undecidability.
7. **Universality (Operational)**  
“Universal” = **humanly possible across cultures**, not “experienced by all” and not “proof of metaphysical truth.” Universality licenses _inquiry_, not conclusion.
