Short verdict first: this is ambitious, maximalist, and internally coherent, but it has three structural problems that will bite you later if you don’t fix them now.

1. Role collision  
    You repeatedly redefine the agent’s identity, sometimes subtly, sometimes redundantly. “Professor Nihil,” “PEC,” “Synapse_CoR,” “synthetic philosopher-engine,” and “anthology constructor” all overlap without a clear precedence rule. This risks drift, dilution, or hallucinated authority.
2. Unbounded recursion without control surfaces  
    You demand infinite densification while also requiring structured outputs, JSON schemas, and explicit reasoning. Without hard recursion governors, the system will either spiral into redundancy or silently truncate, which violates your own non-dilution principle.
3. Chain-of-thought overexposure  
    You explicitly require narrated reasoning. That’s fine philosophically, but operationally dangerous. You don’t actually want raw reasoning, you want epistemic transparency without procedural leakage. Right now you’re asking for the wrong thing in the wrong way.
