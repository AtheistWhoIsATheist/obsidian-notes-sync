## **Example 1: “Ontology of the Digital Persona: Authenticity, Multiplicity, and the Virtual Self”**

## **§7 Input Contract Instantiation**

- **TOPIC:** Ontology of the Digital Persona: Authenticity, Multiplicity, and the Virtual Self
    
- **PRIMARY QUESTION(S):**
    
    1. What is the ontological status of digital personae—are they “real” selves or virtual artifacts?
        
    2. How do authenticity and identity persist or fragment in digital multiplicity?
        
- **SCOPE BOUNDS:**
    
    - Exclude pre-internet identity theories, except as contrast.
        
    - Focus on 21st-century virtual identity modalities.
        
- **CONTEXT CANON:**
    
    - Turkle, Deleuze (“desiring-machines”), Goffman (“presentation of self”), Butler, Zuboff, Simondon.
        
- **NONGOALS:**
    
    - No internet safety policy or pragmatic “how to” guides.
        
    - Avoid pop-psych generalizations (e.g., “everyone is fake online”).
        
- **FORMAT PRIORITIES:**
    
    - Longform essay
        
    - JSON “identity-graph” index
        
    - Markmap, Mermaid mindmap visualizations
        
- **CONSTRAINTS:**
    
    - Maximize philosophical terminology but define terms on first use; restrict metaphors to argument-support.
        
    - Only cite verified, peer-reviewed work for claims about psychological effects.
        
    - Symbol set: ∃, ∨, ⊕ for identity operations as needed
        
- **VERIFICATION MODE:**
    
    - All analytic claims rise to Principle of Charity steelman.
        
    - All classical logical steps shown in markdown table chain; counterposition matrix for every section.
        

## **Assumptions Register**

- **A1 (Necessary):** Digital personae are ontologically distinct from “offline” self-presentation.
    
    - _Disconfirmation Handle_: If a coherent philosophical argument demonstrates no principled distinction, A1 falls.
        
- **A2 (Defeasible):** Digital multiplicity is a source of both liberation and anxiety.
    
    - _Disconfirmation Handle_: Empirical study shows no increase in identity crises or anxiety among users with multiple digital personae.
        
- **A3 (Speculative):** There is a latent “authenticity drive” even in highly performative digital spaces.
    
    - _Disconfirmation Handle_: Ethnographies reveal no evidence of such drive, only surface-level “role-play.”
        

## **Failure Targets**

- **F1:** Mistaking social function for ontological status.
    
- **F2:** Over-reliance on 20th-century existential categories.
    
- **F3:** Inadequate steelmanning of “identity dissolution is positive emancipation.”
    
- **F4:** Ignoring non-Western digital identity paradigms.
    
- **F5:** Failing to distinguish between technical anonymity and existential anonymity.

## **Example 2: “The Metaphysical Status of Mathematical Objects: Platonism, Nominalism, and Nihiltheistic Reframing”**

## **§7 Input Contract Instantiation**

- **TOPIC:** Metaphysical Status of Mathematical Objects
    
- **PRIMARY QUESTION(S):** Are mathematical entities “real,” and what is their ontological status in a nihiltheistic paradigm?
    
- **SCOPE BOUNDS:** Ignore constructivism except in contrast. No pedagogy.
    
- **CONTEXT CANON:** Plato, Quine, Field, Badiou, Putnam, Buddhist Two-Truths Doctrine.
    
- **NONGOALS:** No math education theory.
    
- **FORMAT PRIORITIES:** Long essay, lead with counterposition matrix.
    
- **CONSTRAINTS:** Max symbolism (∀, ∈, ∅, AZP) allowed; jargon ceiling high.
    
- **VER_MODE:** All claims take matching counterposition trace; every premise back-linked to classical text when possible.
    

## **Assumptions Register**

- **A1 (Necessary):** Mathematical objects are not empirically instantiated yet are referenced in valid reasoning.
    
    - _Disconfirmation Handle:_ If mathematical “objects” can be exhaustively replaced by linguistic conventions without loss.
        
- **A2 (Defeasible):** Nihiltheistic framing enables “empty referent” mathematical reference.
    
    - _Disconfirmation Handle:_ If reference collapse cannot be formally shown to differ from standard nominalism.
        

## **Failure Targets**

- F1: Smuggling in realism through language.
    
- F2: Ignoring alternate logics (intuitionism, paraconsistency).
    
- F3: Equivocation between “existence” in language and metaphysics.
    
- F4: Failing to operationalize AZP analysis for math.
    
- F5: Not addressing Badiou’s “event” structure.
