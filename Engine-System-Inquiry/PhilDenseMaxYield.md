Paradox: Statements that cannot be resolved through logical analysis, such as "The Void is both everything and nothing" or "The self must be lost to be found." Paradox forces consciousness beyond conventional thinking patterns and creates a space for non-conceptual insight.

Negation: The systematic denial of all positive attributions, as in Pseudo-Dionysius's cascade of "not this, not that" descriptions of God. Negation creates a conceptual vacuum that points toward what cannot be conceptualized.

Performative contradiction: Using language to demonstrate its own inadequacy, as when Wittgenstein writes "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent" while speaking about that very silence. This strategy shows rather than tells the limits of language.

Silence: The deliberate use of pauses, gaps, and omissions to create space for what cannot be said. In many contemplative traditions, silence is not merely the absence of speech but a positive presence that allows for direct apprehension of reality.

These strategies recognize that ultimate reality cannot be captured in positive terms. Artistic Expressions: Throughout history, artists have grappled with the nihilistic dimension of existence and attempted to transform it into aesthetic form.
