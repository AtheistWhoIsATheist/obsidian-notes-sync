**Core Insight**: "Form is emptiness, emptiness is form" - phenomena neither exist nor non-exist in substantial sense
**PNT Mapping**: 

- **Vertex L** (Language & Void): Śūnyatā doctrine systematically deconstructs all conceptual categories
- **Central Ø** (Lucid Void): Ultimate reality as neither existence nor non-existence

### Madhyamaka Tetralemma Applied to PNT

Traditional Buddhist logic: A, not-A, both A and not-A, neither A nor not-A

**PNT Applications**:

- **Vertex M** (Meaning): Reality has meaning, lacks meaning, both, neither
- **Vertex E** (Ethics): Actions are moral, immoral, both, neither  
- **Vertex N** (Being/Non-being): Better to exist, not exist, both, neither
- **Central Ø**: Trans-tetralemmatic recognition beyond all four positions

**Philosophical Innovation**: Eastern tetralemma logic provides framework for nihiltheistic recognition that transcends conventional dialectical categories without falling into relativism.

## MĀYĀ (ILLUSION/APPEARANCE) - PRIMARY MAPPING TO VERTEX M & E

**Definition**: The apparent reality that conceals ultimate truth (Brahman), not false but relative truth requiring transcendence.

### Advaita Vedanta Framework

**Śaṅkara's Analysis**: 

- Phenomenal world as neither real nor unreal (sadasat-vilakṣaṇa)
- Brahman-Ātman identity beyond subject-object dualism
- Liberation through recognition of illusory separation

**PNT Mapping**:

- **Vertex M** (Meaning): Conventional meaning-structures as māyā requiring transcendence
- **Vertex E** (Ethics): Moral categories as relative truth within illusory framework
- **Central Ø**: Brahman-realization as trans-conceptual recognition

**From J314 Corpus Integration**:

- **Hinduism/Upanishads** (Score: 0.85): "Neti neti" - systematic negation methodology [J314 corpus]
- **Aldous Huxley** (Score: 0.9): Perennial philosophy and psychedelic dissolution of māyā [J314 corpus]

### Māyā vs Nihilistic Meaninglessness

**Key Difference**: 

- Māyā: Phenomenal meaninglessness concealing ultimate meaning (Brahman)
- Nihiltheistic Void: Recognition that may reveal ultimate meaning OR ultimate meaninglessness
- **Integration**: Both require systematic dissolution of conventional categories, but maintain different orientations regarding what dissolution reveals

**Convergent Recognition**: Both traditions discover transcendent significance through rather than despite recognition of conventional reality's constructed character.

## WU (無) - DAOIST NOTHINGNESS - PRIMARY MAPPING TO VERTEX L & N

**Definition**: Creative nothingness that is source of all being; not mere absence but fecund emptiness that enables manifestation.

### Daoist Dialectical Structure

**Dao De Jing Framework**:

- "Dao that can be described is not enduring Dao" - ineffability principle
- Wu wei (non-action) as most effective action through non-interference  
- Being emerges from non-being; form from emptiness

**PNT Mapping**:

- **Vertex L** (Language/Void): Systematic recognition of conceptual inadequacy
- **Vertex N** (Ontic Negation): Creative rather than destructive nothingness
- **All Vertices**: Wu wei as methodology for authentic relationship with reality

**From J314 Corpus Integration**:

- **Taoism** (Score: 0.8): "Tao that can be described is not the enduring and unchanging Tao" [J314 corpus]
- Maps across multiple vertices through systematic non-interference methodology

### Wu Wei and Nihiltheistic Praxis

**Methodological Convergence**:

- **Daoist Wu Wei**: Acting through non-acting; achieving through not-seeking
- **Nihiltheistic Recognition**: Transcendence through rather than despite meaninglessness
- **Practical Integration**: Both require systematic non-attachment to outcome while maintaining authentic engagement

**Innovation**: Daoist wu wei provides practical methodology for nihiltheistic existence - how to continue acting authentically after recognition that action has no ultimate ground or guarantee.

## ANATMAN (NO-SELF) - PRIMARY MAPPING TO VERTEX D & Ø

**Definition**: The doctrine that permanent, substantial self is illusion; what appears as self is process without substantial core.

**Five Skandha Deconstruction**:

- Form, sensation, perception, mental formations, consciousness

- Liberation through recognition of selflessness rather than self-realization

**PNT Mapping**:

- **Vertex D** (Despair/Transcendence): Ego-dissolution as spiritual crisis and breakthrough
- **Central Ø** (Lucid Void): Recognition of substantial self's absence
- **Vertex N** (Ontic Negation): What is there to preserve if no substantial self exists?

**From J314 Corpus Integration**:

- **Buddhism** (Score: 0.9): Systematic no-self analysis [J314 corpus]
- **Ego Dissolution theme**: 25 members including Eckhart, Molinos, Kierkegaard [Previous analysis]

### Anatman vs Western Ego-Dissolution

**Comparative Analysis**:

- **Buddhist Anatman**: Systematic philosophical and meditative investigation of selflessness
- **Christian Mysticism** (Eckhart, John of Cross): Self-negation for divine union

- **Nihiltheistic Recognition**: Self-dissolution revealing either transcendent ground OR confirmed emptiness

**Integration**: All traditions recognize substantial self as constructed, but differ regarding what this recognition reveals about ultimate reality.
