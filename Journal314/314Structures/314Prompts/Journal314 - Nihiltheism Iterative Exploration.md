## I. ONTOLOGICAL MAP OF EXISTENTIAL ANXIETY

Existential anxiety is not a singular emotional state but an ontological condition—a revealing of Being by way of its unraveling. Across traditions, this anxiety is not mere fear of an object, but the abyssal condition in which all objects dissolve. Below, we extract the phenomenological texture of this dread from key thinkers:

---

### 1.1 ANXIETY – The Ontological Rending of Security

- **Søren Kierkegaard**:
	> “Anxiety is the dizziness of freedom.”  
	> *Freedom is not empowering—it is destabilizing. The subject stands suspended between infinite possibilities, paralyzed before the yawning openness of potential. This anxiety is a signal that meaning must be constructed, but offers no tools with which to construct it.*
- **Martin Heidegger**:
	> “Anxiety reveals the nothing.”  
	> *Unlike fear, which is tied to specific entities, anxiety is unbound—it strips away the world as meaningful. In this mood, Dasein encounters the nullity of all beings and the nothing that grounds Being. The world becomes uncanny (unheimlich), familiar-yet-alien.*
- **Buddha (Dukkha)**:
	> “All conditioned things are impermanent—when one sees this with wisdom, one turns away from suffering.”  
	> *Dukkha is not mere suffering, but the total impermanence of existence. Anxiety arises from clinging to the illusion of permanence—of self, identity, and continuity. Letting go births transcendence, but first requires the confrontation with loss and flux.*
- **Albert Camus**:
	> “The absurd is born of this confrontation between the human need and the unreasonable silence of the world.”  
	> *Here, anxiety emerges from ontological mismatch. The universe is mute, but man demands meaning. Camus refuses transcendence, yet recognizes this disjunction as the core human experience—the absurd as anguish.*
- **William James**:
	> “Our normal waking consciousness… is but one special type of consciousness, while all about it, parted from it by the filmiest of screens, there lie potential forms of consciousness entirely different.”  
	> *Anxiety is triggered when the 'normal' dissolves and alternate realities press in. James implies that consciousness itself is fragile—just one layer among many, barely keeping chaos at bay.*
- **Lev Shestov**:
	> “The highest freedom is the freedom from necessity.”  
	> *Anxiety is not eradicated through rational control—it is deepened by the awareness that necessity (law, reason, structure) may be illusory. The freedom to fall into the irrational terrifies rather than liberates.*
- **Paul Tillich**:
	> “Anxiety is the state in which a being is aware of its possible nonbeing.”  
	> *Tillich merges the existential with the metaphysical: anxiety is ontological—it is the psychic echo of the possibility of ceasing to be.*
- **Timothy Leary**:
	> “You are not the target of the universe. The universe doesn’t give a damn about you.”  
	> *Cosmic indifference is not just a proposition but an existential trauma. Leary strips away the anthropocentric illusion—provoking vertigo, detachment, and transcendence simultaneously.*
