**Article Title:** Timothy Leary's Five Levels Of Psychedelic Experience

**Author:** Luke Sholl, Veteran Cannabis Journalist & CBD Expert

**Overview:** The article discusses Timothy Leary's five levels of the psychedelic experience, offering a guide to what one might expect when taking psychedelic drugs. It emphasizes the importance of preparation ("set and setting") for a positive experience.

**Timothy Leary's Five Levels:**

*   **Level One Psychedelic Experience:**
    *   **Effects:** Light synesthesia (mingling of the senses), heightened awareness (especially of music). The world feels familiar.
    *   **Drugs:** Cannabis

*   **Level Two Psychedelic Experience:**
    *   **Effects:** Warping, breathing, and other visual effects; strong emotional responses; light closed-eye visuals (CEVs).
    *   **Drugs:** Light doses of psilocybin, light doses of peyote, medium doses of MDMA.

*   **Level Three Psychedelic Experience:**
    *   **Effects:** Fractals, tracers, and strong visual hallucinations; deep thoughts about the self and the universe; thought loops; high sensitivity to physical stimuli; light visuals and auditory disorder; uncontrollable laughter.
    *   **Drugs:** Regular doses of psilocybin, regular doses of LSD, regular doses of peyote, regular doses of "the 2Cs," low-medium doses of Salvia divinorum.

*   **Level Four Psychedelic Experience:**
    *   **Effects:** Powerful, otherworldly visuals; visions; dissolution of reality; out-of-body experiences; profound realizations.
    *   **Drugs:** High doses of psilocybin, LSD, peyote, the 2Cs, and salvia.

*   **Level Five Psychedelic Experience:**
    *   **Effects:** Ego death; communication with otherworldly beings; total eradication of reality; very little difference between having eyes open or closed; propulsion at great speed; audiences with god-like beings; strangely sober, rational mental state.
    *   **Drugs:** DMT, extremely high doses of other psychedelic drugs.

**Additional Notes:**

*   The article states that the five levels are not strict boundaries and that one can easily transition between them.
