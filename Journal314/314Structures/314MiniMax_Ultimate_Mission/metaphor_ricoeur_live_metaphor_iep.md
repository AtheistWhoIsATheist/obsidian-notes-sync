**3. Existential Phenomenology: Paul Ricoeur, Hermeneutics, and Metaphor**

*   **Overview:** Ricoeur revisits Husserlian and Heideggerian themes in his treatment of metaphor. He develops a discursive theory of symbol, focusing on symbols in religious texts and sacred narratives. He sees metaphor as a re-appropriation of our effort to exist.
*   **Key Idea:** Metaphor must include the notion that such language is expressive and constitutive of the being of those who embark on philosophical reflection.
*   **Central Claim:** "the symbol gives rise to the thought."
*   **Method:** Ricoeur centers the creation of meaning in the existential context, reflecting on experience. The noetic act originates in the encounter with a living text.

**a. The Mechanics of Conceptual Blending**

*   **Focus:** Signifying power of metaphor lies primarily at the sentence level, not individual terms. It is a discursive act which achieves its purpose through extended predication rather than simple substitution.
*   **Theory of Language:** Works through a structure of double reference.
*   **Category Mistake:** Ricoeur reads metaphorical transfer as a kind of "category mistake," which produces an imaginative construction about new ways objects may be related to one another.
*   **Semantic Gap:** A "nameless act" is a kind of "cognitive leap" since there is no conventional term for it. This gap results in a new meaning association, creating a metaphor that signals the larger discursive context.
*   **Role of Metaphor:** The figurative acts as a hermeneutic medium that negates and displaces the original term, signifying a "new kind of object," a new form of being (*logos*).
*   **Outcome:** The metaphorical statement allows us to say that an object *is and is not* what we usually call it.
