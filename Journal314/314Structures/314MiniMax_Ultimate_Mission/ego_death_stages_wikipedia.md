**4. Psychological and Mystical Perspectives:**

*   **Western Mysticism:**
    *   Conceptualization of mystical union as the death of the ego.
    *   Rooted in Roman Catholic teachings (St. Teresa of Ávila) and the *fana* of Islamic Sufis.
*   **Jungian Psychology:**
    *   "Psychic death" is a synonym for "ego death," implying a fundamental transformation of the psyche.
    *   Involves a unification of archetypal opposites through conscious suffering.
    *   Related to St. John of the Cross' *Ascent of Mt. Carmel* and *Dark Night of the Soul*.
*   **Buddhism:**
    *   Zen practice leads to ego-death ("great death").
    *   Meditation is the practice of "forgetting" the sense of self.
    *   David Loy highlights fear of egolessness and the need to undergo ego-death to realize true nature
    *   *Anatta* (non-self) is a key doctrine, and not synonymous with ego death.
*   **Taoism:**
    *   Bruce Frantzis describes an experience of "ru ding" (fear of ego annihilation) during Tai Chi practice.
    *   The ego reacts with fear and resistance when the connection to the universal life force is threatened.
*   **Bernadette Roberts:**
    *   Distinguishes between "no ego" (prior to the unitive state) and "no self" (after the unitive state).
    *   Defines "ego" as the immature self.
    *   Defines "self" as the totality of consciousness.
*   **A Course in Miracles:**
    *   "The ego's death is your life."
    *   The ego is presented as an illusion that disappears when one lets it go.
