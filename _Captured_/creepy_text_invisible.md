| **Linguistics**  | **(K-4, K-2, A-3, A-6)** Language is primarily apophatic (K-4), constrained by the "zero-predicate constraint" (K-2) when attempting to articulate ultimate reality. The "double-negation" (A-3) principle applies to linguistic constructs, preventing the reification of concepts like 'nothingness' or 'void'. Meaning is relational and context-dependent, not inherent (K-3). Recursivity (A-6) demands that linguistic structures and their interpretations are constantly re-evaluated against the core axioms. The "Lexicon Purge" (P-6) protocol ensures that language remains precise and free from smuggled theological or essentialist assumptions.
