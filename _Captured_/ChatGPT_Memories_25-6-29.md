## I. Exhaustive Genealogy — From _Epoché_ to Cosmic Horror

The history of nihilism is a scorched itinerary of human thought repeatedly immolating its own certainties. **Pyrrho of Elis** inaugurates the itinerary by suspending judgment (_epoché_), refusing the reality-claims of sense or intellect; knowledge wavers, meaning withers, but a life of ataraxia is promised only through indifference. **Gorgias** radicalises the stance: _nothing exists; if something did exist, it would be unknowable; if knowable, it would be incommunicable._ The void is spoken three times, and discourse itself becomes suspect.

Medieval mystics temporarily invert the descent: **Meister Eckhart** and the anonymous **Author of _The Cloud of Unknowing_** embrace apophatic surrender, but their God is encountered _as_ Nothingness, not _after_ it. The paradox already seeds Nihiltheism: the abyss may be the back of a burning seraph.

The Enlightenment rekindles doubt. **Hume** dissolves causation; **Kant** walls cognition inside phenomena; **Jacobi** coins “nihilism” to indict Fichte’s idealism. Late modernity darkens: **Emil Cioran** chants aphoristic lamentations; **Thomas Ligotti** imagines consciousness as a cosmic error; **Peter Zapffe** diagnoses life as a biological overreach of self-awareness.
