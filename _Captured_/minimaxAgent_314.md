Appendix A: Core Nihiltheistic Lexicon (Selected Terms)

Universal Nihilistic Event (UNE): The core phenomenological rupture where consciousness confronts fundamental groundlessness. Modalities: Worldly (immanent meaninglessness) and Transcendent (void as opening).

Nihiltheism: A meta-framework recognizing the nihilistic void as potentially revelatory of an apophatically understood Transcendent.

Hermeneutic Ignorance: Misinterpretation of UNE accounts due to a lack of firsthand experiential grounding by the interpreter.

Solutionist Displacement: The premature resolution or domestication of the void’s radical implications into closed systems (doctrinal, therapeutic).

Spiritual Undertow: The subtle, persistent pull towards deeper existential inquiry following an initial void-encounter.

Apophatic Language: Language that describes the Transcendent by negation, emphasizing what it is not, due to its ineffable nature.

Groundless Ground: A paradoxical term indicating that the ultimate ‘foundation’ of reality is itself without foundation, i.e., the void.

Void-Tolerance: The capacity to remain present to and explore the experience of groundlessness without immediate defensive retreat or conceptual closure.

Ontological Corruption (Calvin): The idea that human nature is fundamentally distorted at its core, a “factory of idols.”

Ungrund (Böhme): The groundless ground; the primordial, indeterminate abyss from which reality emerges.

Śūnyatā (Nagarjuna): Emptiness; the doctrine that all phenomena lack inherent existence (svabhāva).

The Neutral (Blanchot): That which lies beyond the opposition of being and non-being, an indifferent space.

Il Y A (Levinas): “There is”; the anonymous, impersonal, horrifying persistence of being without justification.

The Real (Lacan): That which is impossible to symbolize, the traumatic kernel resisting integration into the symbolic order.

(This is a small selection from user_input_files/Lexicon & Categories of Nihiltheism.txt and concepts developed throughout the project.)
