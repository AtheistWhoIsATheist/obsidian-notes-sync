## 🔧 DESIGN NOTES FOR THE HUMAN

### Architectural Decisions (Why This Is Built This Way)

1. **Hierarchical Structural Expansion**
- The prompt is modular so you can remove entire blocks without breaking the rest.
- It also supports “progressive disclosure”: you can start minimal and toggle deeper QA and risk handling later.
1. **Preflight Contract**
- Most failures happen because the assistant guesses what you meant. Preflight makes the assistant say what it thinks it’s doing before it does it.
1. **Strong Definitions**
- Definitions are binding because prompts fail when words become vibes.
- The “Key Terminology” section prevents quiet drift in meaning across long exchanges.
1. **Scope + Redlines**
- Scope controls prevent accidental role-bloat.
- Redlines prevent “creative compliance” where the model technically answers but causes harm or liability.
1. **Reasoning + Epistemic Labels**
- This is an anti-hallucination scaffold.
- Labels force the model to distinguish what it knows from what it’s guessing.
1. **Failure-Mode-First Design**
- Models do weird things under stress: ambiguity, contradictions, forbidden tasks.
- Templates for failure modes keep behavior predictable and safe.
1. **QA Gates**
- A prompt without QA is basically a wish.
- Gates turn “do a good job” into checkable conditions.
1. **Batch Support**
- Real users ask for bundles. Batch mode makes multi-task requests manageable without losing constraints.
