- This relentless pursuit of activities is a fundamental attempt to fill or mask the void, but it obstructs the true nature of Nihilism, as humans desperately try to sustain meaning in life despite its inherent futility.
- The seduction of assigning importance to everyday life and its activities is a cancerous illusion that obstructs a clear view of the inherent lack of meaning, and humans tend to find solace in superficialities like personal relationships, achievements, and societal progress.
- Many individuals retreat into shallow interpretations of religious language, family relations, or transient worldly pleasures, systematically avoiding an authentic confrontation with the self, which is a profound form of self-deception, as observed in the concept of "pathetic positivity".
- The pervasive optimism in human interaction and activity is vehemently critiqued as an avoidance mechanism, preventing true philosophical inquiry, and true philosophers, like children, relentlessly ask "Why?", whereas the majority are merely "unthinking robots" regurgitating prepackaged stories.
