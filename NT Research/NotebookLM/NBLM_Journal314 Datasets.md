The Void is thus paradoxically articulated as simultaneously empty and full, absent and present.

For the mystic, the collapse of fixed ideas about the divine is mandatory; one must abandon every finite conception, realizing that the ultimate goal is liberation from the conceptualized God, allowing the experience of the reality that is beyond existence itself ("I pray God to rid me of God").

• Apophatic Necessity: Because the underlying reality—the Transcendent Nothingness—defies human categorization, Nihiltheism necessitates an apophatic discourse. Language, evolved to describe the finite world, proves inadequate when confronted with the boundless, rendering experiences ineffable and demanding reliance on poetic metaphor, paradox, and contemplative silence.
