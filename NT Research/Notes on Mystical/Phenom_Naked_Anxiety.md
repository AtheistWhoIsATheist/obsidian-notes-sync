## I. Signature Passages: Raw Existential Exposure

### A. Cioran

> “Anxiety is not provoked: it tries to find a justification for itself, and in order to do so seizes upon anything, the vilest pretexts, to which it clings once it has invented them.... \[A\]nxiety is a fever of subjectivity... For no reason, we are prey to agitations, to woundings which no blood will ever staunch.”  
> — [Beyond the Rhetoric](https://btr.michaelkwan.com/2019/04/21/sunday-snippet-emil-cioran/)

> “We do not rush toward death, we flee the catastrophe of birth, survivors struggling to forget it. Fear of death is merely the projection into the future.”  
> —

### B. Kierkegaard (as interpreted by comparison articles)

> “Anxiety may be compared with dizziness. He whose eye happens to look down the yawning abyss becomes dizzy. But what is the reason for this? It is just as much in his own eye as in the abyss. Anxiety is the dizziness of freedom, which emerges when the spirit wants to posit the synthesis and freedom looks down into its own possibility...”  
> — ([View of The Kierkegaardian and Heideggerian Anxiety](https://bcpublication.org/index.php/EP/article/view/2683/2654))

### C. Tillich

> “The courage to be is rooted in the God who appears when God has disappeared in the anxiety of doubt...  
> The basic anxiety, the anxiety of a finite being about the threat of non-being, cannot be eliminated. It belongs to existence itself.”  
> —

> “The three forms he discovered were: anxiety of fate and death, anxiety of emptiness and meaninglessness, and anxiety of guilt and condemnation.”  
> — [Eric Hyde’s Blog: Tillich on the 3 forms of anxiety](https://ehyde.wordpress.com/2019/08/26/paul-tillich-on-the-3-forms-of-anxiety/)

### D. Heidegger (via comparative summaries)

> “Anxiety reveals the nothing... Anxiety in the face of nothingness discloses the world as just 'there,' without reference or meaning.”  
> — (Paraphrased/extracted from [same comparative discussion as Kierkegaard](https://bcpublication.org/index.php/EP/article/view/2683/2654))

### E. Ligotti

> “This is the great lesson the story of the universe teaches: Nothing; everything comes and goes, everything dies, everything vanishes, everything ends – everything is ultimately meaningless.”  
> “No self now, consciously speaking. No feeling your old self or new self, false imaginings if you think about it, self-conscious nothings everywhere you look.”  
> —

### F. Zapffe

> “One night in long bygone times, man awoke and saw himself. He saw that he was naked under cosmos, homeless in his own body. All things dissolved before his testing thought: wonder above wonder, terror above terror, unfolded in his mind. The price for self-consciousness is anxiety and the burden of knowing the absurd; a refusal to participate in the comedy of life.”  
> — [Wikiquote: Peter Wessel Zapffe](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Peter_Wessel_Zapffe) and [StudioQ: The Last Messiah](https://studioq.com/blog/2023/4/19/the-last-messiah-peter-zapffe)

### G. Cross-Tradition Mystics / Apophatic Tradition

> “Sooner or later, however, psychic fatigue sets in; the state of illumination begins to break up, the complementary negative consciousness appears, and shows itself as an overwhelming sense of darkness and deprivation.”  
> — [Value of Sparrows: Underhill on Dark Night](https://thevalueofsparrows.wordpress.com/2012/03/26/mysticism-the-dark-night-of-the-soul-with-help-from-evelyn-underhill/)

> “On a dark night,  
> Inflamed by love-longing  
> — O exquisite risk! —  
> Undetected I slipped away.  
> My house, at last, grown still.  
> Secure in the darkness, I climbed...”  
> — [Goodreads: Dark Night of the Soul by John of the Cross](https://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/3109870-la-noche-oscura-del-alma)
