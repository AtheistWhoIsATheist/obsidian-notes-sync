## Introduction to Nihiltheism and the Hyperontological Void

- The concept of Uncanniness as the Negative Sacrament of Revelation is rooted in the philosophical and theological system of Nihiltheism, which posits that the Transcendent is the Hyperontological Void, a "groundless ground" that exceeds the categories of Being and Nothing, and is revealed through the collapse of ontological stability and meaning, a process known as active nihilation.
- In Nihiltheism, anxiety, or Angst, is recontextualized from a psychological pathology to be cured into the primary phenomenological mechanism of divine revelation, functioning as the specific operation that dismantles the illusions of stable existence, thereby allowing the Transcendent to disclose itself.
- The phenomenological mechanism of anxiety operates by distinguishing itself from fear, with fear targeting objects and reinforcing the "world of things", while anxiety targets existence itself, bypassing the realm of things and engaging directly with the "ontological" structure of existence.
- The primary operation of anxiety is the disintegration of the "referential totality", the invisible network of meanings, tools, and social roles that makes the world intelligible, leading to a "meaning drain" where objects lose their significance and a "defamiliarization" of the world, rendering it radically strange.
