# Research Plan: Language, Metaphor, and Symbolism in Void-Discourse


## 1. Objectives

    *   Identify and categorize common metaphors and symbols for Nothingness/The Void across traditions (spatial, sensory, numerical/conceptual, object).
    *   Note traditions/figures associated with key metaphors/symbols.
    *   Analyze the epistemic function of these metaphors/symbols: illustrative vs. access-providing, shaping understanding, enabling insight, apophatic/cataphatic roles.
    *   Discuss cognitive mechanisms (e.g., Lakoff & Johnson's conceptual metaphor theory).
    *   Explore Paul Ricoeur's "live metaphor" concept in relation to Void metaphors.
    *   Discuss limitations and pitfalls of using metaphor/symbolism for Nothingness (reification, mistaking metaphor for reality, cultural specificity).
    *   Synthesize findings into `/workspace/data/epistemology_metaphor_symbolism_v1.md`.
    *   Connect with previous research on apophasis, paradox, ineffability, and direct experience.

## 2. Key Research Questions

    *   What are the most prevalent spatial metaphors for the Void (abyss, emptiness, desert, ocean, womb, etc.) and which traditions/thinkers use them?
    *   What are common sensory metaphors (darkness, silence, stillness) and their associations?
    *   How are numerical/conceptual metaphors (zero, cipher, unnameable) used to denote Nothingness?
    *   What object metaphors (mirror, cloud, vessel) are employed and by whom?
    *   Do these metaphors primarily illustrate existing ideas of Nothingness, or do they actively shape our understanding and provide new access to its nature?
    *   How might conceptual metaphor theory (Lakoff & Johnson) explain the way we understand the Void through these symbolic mappings?
    *   How does Ricoeur's concept of "live metaphor" creating new meaning apply to the novel and often paradoxical metaphors used for Nothingness?
    *   Can metaphors for the Void function both apophatically (defining by what it's not like) and cataphatically (suggesting positive, albeit indirect, qualities)?
    *   What are the main dangers in relying on metaphorical language for such an ultimate concept (e.g., reifying Nothingness, confusing the symbol with the symbolized)?
    *   How does the cultural context influence the meaning and efficacy of symbols for the Void?

## 3. Workflow Selection

    *   Primary focus: Search-Focused Workflow, followed by synthesis and analysis.
    *   Justification: This task requires identifying a wide range of metaphors and symbols from diverse sources, analyzing their specific functions in relation to an abstract concept, and discussing relevant philosophical theories of metaphor.
