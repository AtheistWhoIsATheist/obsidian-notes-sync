>• Is the "ineffable presence" (your definition) an inevitable phenomenological fact of the encounter, which Heisman simply did not reach?

>• Or, is it a choice? A Kierkegaardian "leap" ?

>• The Journal314 texts suggest it is a revelation (a "grace") that happens to the seeker once the self is sufficiently annihilated (Molinos , Eckhart ). This aligns with your own Platonic insight: the "transcendent experience" is a vision of the Forms —a vision that is given to the philosopher who, having left the cave, is finally able to see.

3. The synthesis of Journal314 thus provides the evidence: Nihilism is the crucible, and the "transmutation" is the Great Work. The texts show that the difference between the modern pessimist and the ancient mystic is not the experience of the Void, but the capacity to recognize it as the face of God.
