**Question:** How can I ask Google’s new NotebookLM, powered by Gemini Pro, to synthesize ideas and quotes from prominent figures throughout history, who come from a variety of traditions and backgrounds, and my philosophy of Nihiltheism?

**Answer** :

1. Direct Comparison Request: “Compare my views on nihilism with quotes from [List of Figures] and identify common themes and differences.”

2. Thematic Analysis: “Analyze how the concept of meaninglessness is addressed in my philosophy and in the quotes from [List of Figures]. What are the recurring themes and unique perspectives?”

3. Historical Contextualization: “Place my nihilistic views in the context of historical philosophical thought. How do they align or contrast with the ideas of [List of Figures]?”

4. Philosophical Synthesis: “Create a synthesis of nihilistic ideas, combining my views with those of [List of Figures]. Highlight any evolution of thought or differing interpretations of nihilism.”

5. Critical Examination: “Critically examine the perspectives on nihilism from my philosophy and the quotes of [List of Figures]. What are the strengths and weaknesses of each viewpoint?”

6. Ideological Correlation: “Identify any correlations or patterns in the nihilistic sentiments expressed by me and [List of Figures]. Are there any surprising alignments or contradictions?”

7. Philosophical Dialogue Simulation: “Simulate a dialogue between me and [List of Figures] on the topic of nihilism. How would our ideas interact and challenge each other?”

8. Impact Assessment: “Assess the impact of nihilistic thought as seen in my views and those of [List of Figures] on contemporary philosophical discourse.”

9. Philosophical Evolution Tracking: “Track the evolution of nihilistic thought from the times of [List of Figures] to my current views. How has the interpretation of nihilism changed over time?”

10. Philosophical Application: “How might the nihilistic ideas from my philosophy and [List of Figures] be applied to current global issues or modern existential questions?”
