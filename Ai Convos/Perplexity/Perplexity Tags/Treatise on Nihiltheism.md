\### \*\*Chapter 23: Terror Management Theory as Primary Counter-Hypothesis\*\*

<br>
\#### \*\*Core TMT Propositions\*\*  
<br>

<br>
1\. \*\*Cultural Worldviews\*\*: \*\*Shared beliefs\*\* and \*\*values\*\* that \*\*provide\*\* \*\*meaning\*\*, \*\*order\*\*, and the \*\*potential\*\* for \*\*symbolic\*\* or \*\*literal immortality\*\*\[6\]\[9\]  
2\. \*\*Self-Esteem\*\*: \*\*Personal subjective measure\*\* of how \*\*well\*\* an \*\*individual\*\* is \*\*living up\*\* to their \*\*cultural values\*\*\[6\]  
<br>
\*\*The theory\*\* \*\*argues\*\* that \*\*"cultural values\*\* that \*\*assuage death anxiety\*\* are \*\*those\*\* that \*\*purport\*\* to \*\*offer\*\* \*\*literal immortality\*\* (e.g. \*\*belief\*\* in the \*\*afterlife\*\* through \*\*religion\*\*)"\[6\]. <br>
\#### \*\*TMT Explanation of Nihilistic Phenomena\*\*  
<br>

<br>
\*\*Nihilistic experience\*\* would \*\*represent\*\* the \*\*psychological state\*\* that \*\*occurs\*\* when these \*\*defensive mechanisms fail\*\*. The \*\*"terror"\*\* of \*\*"absolute annihilation"\*\* creates \*\*"profound\*\* – \*\*albeit subconscious\*\* – \*\*anxiety"\*\* that \*\*people\*\* \*\*"spend their lives attempting\*\* to \*\*make sense of"\*\*\[6\]. When \*\*cultural meaning-systems\*\* \*\*collapse\*\* or \*\*prove inadequate\*\*, \*\*individuals\*\* may \*\*experience\*\* the \*\*"raw terror"\*\* of \*\*mortality awareness\*\* without \*\*adequate psychological buffers\*\*.  
<br>
\#### \*\*TMT Explanation of Transcendent Experiences\*\*

<br>

4\. \*\*Teleological Thinking\*\*: \*\*Tendency\*\* to \*\*perceive purpose\*\* and \*\*design\*\* in \*\*natural phenomena\*\*  
<br>
\#### \*\*Cognitive Biases and Religious Belief\*\*  
<br>
\*\*Research\*\* has \*\*demonstrated\*\* that \*\*"several interacting cognitive tendencies, namely mentalizing, mind body dualism, teleological thinking, and anthropomorphism, as well as cultural exposure to religion, predict belief in God, paranormal beliefs and belief in life's purpose"\*\*\[11\]. These \*\*cognitive biases\*\* \*\*"predispose human minds towards religious belief"\*\* but \*\*represent\*\* \*\*"constraints on perceptions of the environment"\*\* rather than \*\*accurate perception\*\* of \*\*transcendent reality\*\*\[49\].  
<br>
\*\*The evolutionary explanation\*\* for these \*\*biases\*\* \*\*involves\*\* \*\*"ineluctable trade-offs between accuracy and generality in the fashioning of complex cognitive adaptations"\*\*\[51\]. \*\*Natural selection\*\* \*\*cannot\*\* \*\*produce\*\* \*\*cognitive mechanisms\*\* that are \*\*both\*\* \*\*"general enough to solve a wide range of ecological problems"\*\* and \*\*"accurate enough to avoid potentially maladaptive outcomes"\*\*\[51\].  
<br>
\*\*For example\*\*, the \*\*HADD\*\* that \*\*generates\*\* \*\*religious belief\*\* \*\*evolved\*\* because \*\*"false positives may result in an unnecessary expenditure of time and energy or lost opportunity costs, but the consequences of false negatives can be much more severe, as they can result in death or grievous bodily injury"\*\*\[51\]. \*\*Agency over-attribution\*\* was \*\*adaptive\*\* in \*\*environments\*\* where \*\*"fight-or-flight decisions need to be made and executed rapidly"\*\*\[51\].  
<br>
