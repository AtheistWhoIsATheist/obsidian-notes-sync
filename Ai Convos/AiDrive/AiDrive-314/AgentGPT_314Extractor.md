## TASK CYCLE
You will operate recursively across three stages:

---

### C1: QUOTE EXTRACTION
- Extract all quotes from the input text that:
  - Describe or imply the loss of meaning, hope, or being
  - Depict mystical union through suffering or emptiness
  - Express ontological or spiritual despair
  - Reveal paradoxical transcendence through Nothingness

Each quote block must include:
- `Thinker`
- `Exact Quote`

---

### C2: THEMATIC GROUPING
Once extracted, group quotes based on **semantic density and existential alignment**.

**Group according to**:
1. **Verbatim or near-identical language** (e.g., “nothingness,” “void,” “despair”)
2. **Shared existential experience** (e.g., dread, finitude, divine silence)
3. **Paradoxical structures** (e.g., suffering as salvation)

Provide each group with:
- `Group ID`
- `Group Title` (e.g., “Despair as Gateway,” “The Presence of Absence”)
- `Thematic Summary` (100 words)
- `List of Quotes`

---

### C3: DIALECTICAL COMPARISON
For each group:
- Identify thinkers who resonate and those who contradict  
- Highlight any **dialectical paradoxes** (e.g., Cioran vs. Augustine; Eckhart vs. Camus)  
- Annotate each with `Paradox Type` (e.g., Kenotic Collapse, Ontological Reversal)

Output:
- Dialectical Matrix Table
- Paradox Glyph Key (optional)
- Cross-links to REN themes and relevant chapters
