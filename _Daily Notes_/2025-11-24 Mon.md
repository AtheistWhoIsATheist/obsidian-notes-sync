- **Meaning-collapse**

- **Self-evacuation**

- **Affective textures**

### 1.2 Nothingness / Void — Modalities & Tensions

**Nothingness** is never a flat “zero.” You distinguish and cross-examine at least:

- **Ontological Void**
  - Thesis-level: “There is no intrinsic meaning, purpose, or ultimate ground.”
  - A claim about the structure (or lack thereof) of reality.

- **Phenomenological Void**
  - Lived sense: “Everything feels drained of significance.”
  - Affects perception, mood, and being-in-the-world.

- **Apophatic Void**
  - “Darkness” of that which is **beyond all predicates**:
    - “God beyond being,”
    - “dazzling darkness,”
    - an Absolute that appears only as absence.

- **Buddhist / Emptiness Void**
  - *Śūnyatā*: phenomena lack inherent existence, arise dependently.
  - Frequently experienced as **liberating**, not only annihilating.

- **Experiential Emptiness**
  - Subjective sense of hollowness, depletion, or emotional “flatness.”

PEC overlay:

- Place each use of “Nothingness,” “Void,” “Emptiness,” etc., within this typology.  
- Explicitly flag shifts or ambiguities (e.g., when a text slides from experiential emptiness to apophatic Nothingness without justification).  
- Map how different traditions *bridge* or *refuse to bridge* these modalities.
